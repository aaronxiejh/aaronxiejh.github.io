Research

Dissertation: Big Data-Based Health Risk Analytics: A Deep Learning Approach


Value-based healthcare is an emerging healthcare delivery model which incentivizes and rewards physicians for improved patient outcomes and quality of care, rather than the amount of services. The objective of value-based healthcare is to move the healthcare delivery system from reactive disease treatment to a proactive care approach. Health risk analytics serves as the analytical foundation for such proactive care by actively assessing various health risks. My research develops novel deep learning frameworks for health risk analytics using big data, including clinical claims, wearable sensors, and social media. The proposed frameworks provide a holistic solution for multi-faceted health risks in three progressive yet integrated levels:

  • Patient behavioral risk: understand risk factors of medication adherence and opioid addiction behaviors
  • Disease risk: predict major disease risks using wearable sensors and clinical claims
  • Policy risk: predict readmission risk for patients and hospitals
